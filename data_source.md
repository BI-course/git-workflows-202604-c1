# Research on Data Sources

A data source is the origin from which data is obtained, eg Databases
Data sources serve as the foundation for data analysis because it provides raw facts and figures that drive research.


## Categories of data sources
Data sources can be categorized into two, namely:
* **1. Primary data:** This is first hand information that is collected for a specific purpose. Examples of this could be, surveys and questionnaires, interviews, observations and experiments.
* **2. Secondary data:** This type of data has already been collected by others(existing). A good example of secondary data is: articles,research papers and government publications.

## Internal and External data sources
 * **Internal Data Source** 
Here, data is generated within the organization. Internal data shows what is happening inside the organization. It is considered the most reliable. Examples include: customer databases, financial records, sales and transaction records.
* **External Data Source** 
External data comes from outside the organization as the name suggests. It can help organizations to understand the external environment as well as benchmark performance against competitors. Examples are: Public data like government reports, market trends ad even industry reports.

## Importance of data sources
Data sources are important because they play a vital role in businesses by enabling managers to make informed decisions.
They also ensure strategic planning is done by providing insights that help organizations long term.
Additionally, data sources are essentail for performance evaluation, they allow businesses to measure success and monitor the business as well as identify areas of improvement.

## Challenges of data sources
Despite data sources playinga crucial role in businesses, they also present certain challenges to companies includng:
1. The quality of the data and accuracy issues.
2. The high cost of collecting primary data 
3. Outdated and irrelevant secondary data
